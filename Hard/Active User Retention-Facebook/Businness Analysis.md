# Business Analysis – Monthly Retained Users

## 📊 What This Metric Represents

This query calculates the number of users who were active in both June and July 2022.

This measures **Month-over-Month Retention**.

Retention is one of the most important KPIs in product analytics.

---

## 🎯 Why Retention Matters

User growth alone does not indicate product success.

A product is considered healthy when:
- Users return consistently
- Engagement remains stable or increases
- Churn remains low

High retention means:
- Strong product-market fit
- Good user experience
- High perceived value

Low retention may indicate:
- Poor onboarding
- Low feature adoption
- Strong competition
- Product dissatisfaction

---

## 📈 Business Interpretation Scenarios

### Scenario 1: High Retention

If 70% of June users return in July:
- Product is sticky
- Engagement strategy is working
- Marketing acquisition quality is strong

### Scenario 2: Low Retention

If only 30% return:
- Users may not find recurring value
- Onboarding may need improvement
- Feature usage analysis required

## 💡 Analyst Insight

Retention is more important than acquisition.

A company growing at 20% but losing 40% of users monthly is unhealthy.

Sustainable growth depends on strong retention.

Understanding retention helps:
- Improve product decisions
- Optimize marketing spend
- Increase long-term revenue
