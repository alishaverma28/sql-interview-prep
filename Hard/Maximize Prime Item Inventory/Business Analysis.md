# Business Analysis – Warehouse Space Optimization

## 📊 What This Problem Represents

This simulates warehouse capacity management.

The company has limited physical space (500,000 sq ft) 
and must decide how to allocate it efficiently.

---

## 🎯 Business Objective

Maximize high-priority inventory (prime_eligible) first.

This reflects real-world strategy:

Prime items:
- Faster shipping
- Higher customer satisfaction
- Higher conversion rate
- Stronger brand loyalty

---

## 📈 Why Prioritize Prime?

Prime customers typically:

- Spend more
- Order frequently
- Expect faster delivery

Storing more prime inventory improves:
- Delivery speed
- Customer retention
- Revenue growth

---

## 🧠 Optimization Logic

This is a constrained optimization problem:

Constraint:
Total warehouse space = 500,000 sq ft

Goal:
Maximize total items stored, with priority logic.

---

## 📉 Business Trade-Off

If too much space is given to non-prime items:

- Prime delivery may slow
- Customer churn may increase

If only prime items are stored:

- Lower-tier customers may face stockouts

Balance is important.

---

## 🚀 Real-World Applications

This logic applies to:

- Inventory allocation
- Capacity planning
- Supply chain optimization
- Retail warehouse management
- E-commerce fulfillment centers

---

## 💡 Executive Insight

Efficient space utilization leads to:

- Lower storage cost per item
- Faster order fulfillment
- Higher operational efficiency
- Better revenue per square foot
