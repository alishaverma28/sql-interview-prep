# Business Analysis – Year-on-Year Growth

## 📊 What This Metric Represents

Year-on-Year (YoY) growth measures how product spend changes compared to the previous year.

It shows the rate at which revenue or demand is increasing or decreasing.

---

## 🎯 Why YoY Growth Is Important

Companies track YoY growth to:

- Measure product performance
- Identify revenue trends
- Evaluate pricing strategies
- Compare seasonal performance
- Forecast future revenue

---

## 📈 Positive YoY Growth

If YoY > 0:

- Product demand is increasing
- Marketing efforts may be effective
- Customer base may be expanding
- Pricing strategy may be successful

Example:
2021 Spend = 1000  
2022 Spend = 1500  
YoY = 50%

This indicates strong growth.

---

## 📉 Negative YoY Growth

If YoY < 0:

- Demand may be declining
- Competitors may be gaining market share
- Product may need improvements
- Pricing may be too high

Example:
2021 Spend = 2000  
2022 Spend = 1500  
YoY = -25%

This signals potential risk.

---

## 🧠 Executive-Level Insight

Leadership uses YoY growth to:

- Make investment decisions
- Allocate marketing budgets
- Identify top-performing products
- Decide product expansion or discontinuation

---

## 📊 Key Metrics Connected to YoY

- Revenue Growth Rate
- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
- Market Share Growth
- Profit Margin Trends
- Sales Performance KPIs

---

## 🚀 Extensions for Advanced Analysis

This can be expanded to:

- Month-over-Month (MoM) growth
- Quarterly growth
- CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)
- Growth by region
- Growth by customer segment
- Growth by acquisition channel

---

## 💡 Analyst Insight

Raw revenue numbers do not tell the full story.

Growth rate shows momentum.

A product with smaller revenue but high YoY growth 
may be more promising than a high-revenue product with declining growth.

Understanding growth trends helps businesses 
make strategic and data-driven decisions.
